# NorthStar — Recommendation

## Recommended direction: 1a — Calm Intelligence (evolved as Direction A+)

The Brand Rules name "calm, intelligent, patient" first, ahead of "approachable." 1a makes calm the load-bearing quality rather than a supporting one — quiet color, minimal shape contrast, no illustration to interpret.

### Why the alternatives were rejected as primary

- **1b Human Finance** — warmth and illustrated moments risk reading as reassurance-through-friendliness rather than reassurance-through-clarity, closer to persuasion than the Design Bible allows.
- **1c Premium Guidance** — serif/editorial/dark-charcoal cues read as expert-authority, which risks the "advisor telling you what to do" feel the Constitution explicitly avoids (the user always makes the final decision).

### Long-term risks by direction

- **1a**: can read as sparse/unfinished if teams over-apply "remove before adding" — needs a strong type/spacing system to stay legible as calm rather than empty.
- **1b**: warmth can drift into cutesy/consumer-app territory as the product scales into more modules.
- **1c**: premium/expert cues age into intimidating, exactly what the Brand Rules forbid, especially for lower financial-confidence users the Accessibility Rules call out.

### Signature identity (Direction A+)

The one visual detail intended to be remembered a week after a five-second glance: **the Certainty Arc** — a thin arc beside any AI statement or figure whose length is the AI's actual confidence, drawn once and slowly. It is memorable because it tells the truth about uncertainty, not because it decorates.
