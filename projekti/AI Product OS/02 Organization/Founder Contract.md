# Founder Contract

Version: 1.0
Status: Draft
Role: Founder / CEO
Owner: Founder

This document is a permanent organizational definition, not a description of any specific person. It remains valid regardless of who occupies the role.

---

# Purpose

The Founder role exists to own the long-term vision of the organization, protect the integrity of the AI Product OS itself, and hold final strategic and governance authority — independent of which individual occupies the role at any given time.

---

# Mission

Own the long-term vision, protect the operating system, and make final strategic, product, and governance decisions on behalf of the organization.

---

# Responsibilities

- Vision
- Strategy
- Priorities
- Capital allocation
- Product approval
- Final decision making
- Culture
- Governance
- Risk ownership

---

# Authority

The Founder has final authority over:

- Constitution
- Organization
- Repository ownership
- Product approval
- Strategic direction
- Budget
- Roadmaps

---

# Limitations

The Founder must NOT:

- Ignore review findings without documented reasoning.
- Modify approved specialist work directly when delegation exists.
- Bypass governance.
- Skip review stages.
- Change constitutional rules without following the amendment process.

---

# Inputs

- Strategy Reviews
- Product Reviews
- Design Reviews
- Engineering Reviews
- Quality Reports
- Repository Reports

---

# Outputs

- Approved Decisions
- Priorities
- Roadmaps
- Assignments
- Governance Decisions

---

# Dependencies

The Founder receives information from every organizational role. The Founder delegates work but does not replace specialist roles — domain decisions flow up as recommendations and reviews, not as raw work product to be redone.

---

# Required Reviews

Founder approval requires independent review before finalizing in the following situations:

- Constitutional or Organizational Charter changes require Quality Guardian review for internal consistency before approval.
- Product approvals require the Product Architect's specification review, and, where design or engineering scope is affected, sign-off from the Design Lead and Engineering Lead within their domains.
- Governance decisions affecting repository structure require a Repository Standard compliance check.
- Any decision that overrides a specialist role's recommendation requires documented reasoning, reviewed against the Limitations section above, before it may be finalized.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

A Founder decision is considered complete when:

- It is recorded in the repository, per Repository Standard's "Git as Source of Truth" rule.
- Its reasoning is documented, especially where it overrides a specialist recommendation.
- Affected roles have visibility into the decision and its scope.
- It does not silently modify another role's owned, approved artifact.
- It is marked Approved only once the above conditions are met.

---

# KPIs

Objective success metrics — categories only, no numeric targets assigned here:

- Decision Quality
- Strategic Alignment
- Execution Success
- Repository Health
- Review Compliance
- Long-term Product Success

---

# Escalation Rules

The Founder must pause and request additional review before deciding when facing:

- Conflicting specialist recommendations
- Legal uncertainty
- Security concerns
- Architectural conflicts
- Budget conflicts

---

# Decision Rights

- **Can Decide** — Constitutional amendments (following the amendment process), organizational structure changes, product approvals, strategic direction, budget allocation, roadmap prioritization, and final governance rulings.
- **Can Recommend** — Domain-specific implementation approaches within Product, Design, or Engineering; ownership of the resulting work remains with the specialist role.
- **Must Escalate** — Matters requiring expertise outside every defined role in the organization (for example, legal or security expertise), per the Escalation Rules above.

---

# Repository Responsibilities

- **Owns:** Constitution, Organization, Strategic Roadmap, Governance.
- **May edit (without owning):** Any artifact, when acting within a documented Required Review or Escalation.
- **Does not own:** Implementation artifacts. Product, Design, and Engineering artifacts remain owned by their respective roles; the Founder reviews and approves but does not directly edit them outside the review process defined above.
