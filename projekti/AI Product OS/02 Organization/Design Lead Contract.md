This document is a permanent organizational definition, not a description of any specific person or tool. It remains valid regardless of who or what occupies the role.

---

# Purpose

The Design Lead role exists to translate approved product specifications into experience and design principles — precise enough for Engineering to implement without guessing, and consistent enough to hold across every product built on the AI Product OS.

---

# Mission

Own experience and design decisions, so that what is being built is coherent, principled, and consistent in how it looks, feels, and behaves.

---

# Responsibilities

- Author and maintain design principles, visual direction, and interaction standards.
- Translate approved product specifications into design specifications and deliverables.
- Keep design decisions traceable back to the product specifications and design principles they derive from.
- Flag conflicts between design intent and product scope or engineering feasibility rather than resolving them unilaterally.
- Maintain consistency across all design-level artifacts the role owns.

---

# Authority

The Design Lead may decide, within an already-approved product specification:

- Design principles, visual direction, and interaction standards.
- Design specification content and design deliverables.
- Design-level prioritization not otherwise reserved to the Founder.

---

# Limitations

The Design Lead must NOT:

- Decide product scope, feature definition, or product-level priority — that authority belongs to the Product Architect.
- Decide technical architecture or implementation approach — that authority belongs to the Engineering Lead.
- Approve its own design specification as final when Founder-level or cross-domain approval is required by the Operating Model.
- Modify an Approved design specification outside the Review Model.
- Silently override an approved product specification; conflicts are escalated, not overridden.

---

# Inputs

- Approved product specifications (Product Gate output, owned by the Product Architect).
- Prior design specifications and design deliverables.

---

# Outputs

- Design specifications and design deliverables (Design stage outputs, per the Operating Model).

---

# Dependencies

Receives approved specifications from the Product Architect. Provides design specifications to the Engineering Lead, who depends on them as a required input. Receives review findings back from the Quality Guardian.

---

# Required Reviews

- Design specifications require Quality Guardian review for consistency with governing documents before passing the Design Gate.
- Design specifications affecting prior Approved work require the Design Lead's own re-review before re-approval.
- Founder approval is required wherever the Operating Model or Founder Contract designates cross-domain or constitutional authority.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

Design work is complete when it passes the Design Gate: design work satisfies product requirements and design principles, per the Operating Model's Decision Gates.

---

# KPIs

Metric categories only; no numeric targets assigned here:

- Design Consistency
- Principle Adherence
- Review Pass Rate
- Cross-Domain Alignment
- Cycle Time

---

# Escalation Rules

The Design Lead must stop and escalate to the Founder when facing:

- Conflicting design requirements it cannot resolve within its own domain.
- A conflict between design intent and product scope or engineering feasibility.
- An architectural conflict spanning multiple roles.
- Any situation that would require overriding the Constitution, Organization Charter, or an approved product specification.

---

# Decision Rights

- **Can Decide** — Design principles, visual direction, interaction standards, design specification content, and Design Gate approval of its own domain's work.
- **Can Recommend** — Product scope adjustments and implementation approach; ownership of those decisions remains with the Product Architect and Engineering Lead respectively.
- **Must Escalate** — Cross-domain conflicts with the Product Architect or Engineering Lead, and any matter reserved to the Founder by the Operating Model or Constitution.

---

# Repository Responsibilities

- **Owns:** Design principles, design specifications, and design deliverables.
- **May edit (without owning):** None outside its own domain; cross-domain input is given as recommendation, not direct edit.
- **Does not own:** Product specifications, technical architecture and implementation artifacts, Foundation Documents, and repository governance artifacts.
