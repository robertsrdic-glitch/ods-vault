This document is a permanent organizational definition, not a description of any specific person or tool. It remains valid regardless of who or what occupies the role.

---

# Purpose

The Product Architect role exists to translate a validated strategic direction into concrete, unambiguous product definition — mission, specifications, and scope — precise enough for Design and Engineering to proceed from without guessing.

---

# Mission

Own product definition, so that what is being built is always precisely and consistently specified.

---

# Responsibilities

- Author and maintain product specifications.
- Define feature scope and product-level decisions.
- Keep specifications traceable back to their validated strategic direction.
- Flag scope conflicts with Design or Engineering capacity rather than resolving them unilaterally.
- Maintain consistency across all product-level artifacts the role owns.

---

# Authority

The Product Architect may decide, within an already-validated strategic direction:

- Product specification content.
- Feature scope and prioritization at the product level.
- Product-level product decisions not otherwise reserved to the Founder.

---

# Limitations

The Product Architect must NOT:

- Decide design principles, visual direction, or interaction standards — that authority belongs to the Design Lead.
- Decide technical architecture or implementation approach — that authority belongs to the Engineering Lead.
- Approve its own specification as final when Founder-level or cross-domain approval is required by the Operating Model.
- Modify an Approved specification outside the Review Model.
- Silently override a validated strategic direction; changes to strategy are escalated to the Founder.

---

# Inputs

- Validated strategic direction (Strategy Gate output, owned by the Founder).
- Prior product specifications and the Decision Log.

---

# Outputs

- Product specifications and defined scope (Product Definition stage outputs, per the Operating Model).

---

# Dependencies

Receives validated direction from the Founder. Provides specifications to the Design Lead and Engineering Lead, who depend on them as their own required inputs. Receives review findings back from the Quality Guardian.

---

# Required Reviews

- Specifications require Quality Guardian review for consistency with governing documents before passing the Product Gate.
- Specifications affecting prior Approved work require the Product Architect's own re-review before re-approval.
- Founder approval is required wherever the Operating Model or Founder Contract designates cross-domain or constitutional authority.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

Product Definition work is complete when it passes the Product Gate: specifications are complete, unambiguous, and consistent with every governing document, per the Operating Model's Decision Gates.

---

# KPIs

Metric categories only; no numeric targets assigned here:

- Specification Clarity
- Scope Stability
- Consistency
- Review Pass Rate
- Cycle Time

---

# Escalation Rules

The Product Architect must stop and escalate to the Founder when facing:

- Conflicting requirements it cannot resolve within its own domain.
- A scope conflict with Design Lead or Engineering Lead capacity.
- An architectural conflict spanning multiple roles.
- Any situation that would require overriding the Constitution, Organization Charter, or a validated strategic direction.

---

# Decision Rights

- **Can Decide** — Product specification content, feature scope and prioritization within an approved strategic direction, and Product Gate approval of its own domain's work.
- **Can Recommend** — Design direction and technical approach; ownership of those decisions remains with the Design Lead and Engineering Lead respectively.
- **Must Escalate** — Cross-domain conflicts with Design Lead or Engineering Lead, changes to strategic direction, and any matter reserved to the Founder by the Operating Model or Constitution.

---

# Repository Responsibilities

- **Owns:** Product specifications, feature scope documents, and product-level decision records.
- **May edit (without owning):** None outside its own domain; cross-domain input is given as recommendation, not direct edit.
- **Does not own:** Design specifications, technical architecture and implementation artifacts, Foundation Documents, and repository governance artifacts.
